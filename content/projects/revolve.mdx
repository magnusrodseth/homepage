## What is Revolve NTNU?

During my first year at Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU), I
was a part of Revolve NTNU. In one year, the team completes a full product
development process in order to produce a capable race car to compete in one of
the largest engineering competitions for students in the world,
[Formula Student](https://no.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formula_Student).

Visit [Revolve NTNU's website](https://www.revolve.no/) to learn more about the
organization.

## My contributions

### Revolve Aerolyze

Personally, I provided **technical solutions** to electrical and mechanical
engineers whilst working in a team. This provided a great opportunity to seek
valuable and **constructive feedback** from my peers.

With a total team size of 5 software engineers, we developed a full-stack web
application called Aerolyze, using **React**, **Next.js**, **Node.js**,
**TypeScript**, **Redux**, **PostgreSQL** and **Azure**.

We used **agile workflows (Kanban, Scrum)** and **Git** version control during
development.

As a user, you download images stored in the cloud (**Azure**) exported from
computer simulations in order to improve the aerodynamics of Revolve NTNU's race
car.

### Revolve Analyze

I contributed to a **data analysis** desktop application called Analyze, using
**C#** and **XAML**. Plotted data using the **SciChart** library.

[Click here for a demo video of Revolve Analyze](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ys4f8toUtiM).

As a user, you can **visualize live data** during live testing of the race car
or plot data from logs stored in the cloud (**Azure PostgreSQL database**)
post-processing.

## Retrospective reflection

Before starting at NTNU, I had only programmed a bit. In particular, I had
played around with some Python, and developed a single static website using
HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I had no experience using some of the most relevant
and useful frameworks.

Thus, joining Revolve NTNU during my first year at NTNU really accelerated my
learning curve. I was thrown into a real-life engineering scenario with little
to no experience beforehand, learning about web development frameworks, agile
workflows and cross-disciplinary teamwork, all at the same time.
